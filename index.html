About

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, IIT Indore, working on logic synthesis and optimization for digital circuits. My research sits at the intersection of formal methods, combinatorial optimization, and electronic design automation.

My work focuses on developing solver-guided optimization techniques for emerging circuit representations—particularly Majority-Inverter Graphs (MIG) and their variants. I design and implement algorithms that improve area, delay, and structural properties of logic networks, with an emphasis on approaches that can be validated through FPGA and ASIC evaluation flows.

Beyond the algorithmic work, I build practical toolchains and benchmarking pipelines that make research outcomes reproducible and comparable. I am drawn to problems where mathematical rigor meets engineering implementation—where a well-formulated optimization objective can translate into measurable gains in real hardware metrics.

I also have industry experience from my time at Synopsys, where I contributed to production-grade EDA tools, grounding my research perspective in the realities of large-scale design workflows.

Selected Projects

Optimization

Solver-Guided MIG Rewriting

Developed an exact synthesis framework using SAT/SMT solvers for optimal MIG rewriting. The approach enumerates structurally minimal representations of Boolean functions within bounded windows, achieving measurable area and depth improvements on EPFL benchmarks.

SAT/SMT MIG C++ ABC
Security

Secure Logic Synthesis with mMIG Transformations

Explored majority-based graph transformations for implementing logic locking and structural obfuscation. Designed synthesis flows using modified MIG (mMIG) representations to enhance resilience against oracle-guided attacks on combinational circuits.

mMIG Logic Locking Python Verilog
Decomposition

Folded-Bias Majority Decomposition

Proposed a decomposition strategy based on folded-bias structures in majority logic, enabling compact representations of functions with biased onset/offset properties. Evaluated against standard decomposition methods for depth and node count.

Majority Logic Decomposition Benchmarking
Tooling

Automated EDA Evaluation Pipeline

Built an end-to-end automation framework for running logic synthesis experiments— covering benchmark ingestion, flow configuration, synthesis execution (Vivado/Yosys), result extraction, and comparative analysis. Designed for reproducibility across different optimization configurations.

Python Vivado Yosys Bash
